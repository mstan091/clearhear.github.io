Hearing Assesments Process

After conducting a physical examination of your ears, we will move on to a series of non-invasive and painless hearing tests that are able to provide answers about the type of hearing loss you are experiencing and its severity.Tests might include:

  • Tympanometry
  • Pure Tone Audiometry
  • Bone Conduction Test
  • Speech Audiometry

As your results are captured in real-time during the testing process, your audiologist will present and discuss your test results during the same visit and they will provide you with a Personalized Hearing Report as well as treatment solutions to overcome your specific hearing challenges or preventative measures if you are at risk of developing a hearing loss.

Ear Wax Removal

Although ear wax production is natural, hearing aid users are far more susceptible to wax buildup than others. Each time a hearing aid is inserted it will push the wax in as it enters, leaving a residual ridge at the end of the hearing aid. Over time this can build up and block the ear canal or fill the sound tube of the hearing aid. Both of these lead to reduced hearing acuity.

Our Audiologists and Hearing Instrument Specialists who are certified in wax management have access to several different systems of ear wax removal depending on the type of wax and the location in your ear canal. The systems that we use are suction, curettes and flushing with water.

Ear wax removal is a service that we provide for all of our existing patients and anyone who uses a hearing aid regardless of where it was purchased.

Custom Ear Plugs

Custom earplugs offer excellent hearing protection. As they are designed specifically for you, they are more involved than if you just grabbed some ready-made earplugs off the shelf. If you are a keen swimmer or musician, exposure to high noise levels is part of your job, so you MUST protect your hearing.

Custom earplugs are designed specifically for you. Foam earplugs are fine if your partner is snoring, but if you're a musician or sound engineer or enjoy swimming, shooting or motorsports, custom earplugs are necessary.

The benefits of custom earplugs are :

  • Customisable and Specially designed
  • Comfortable and Long-lasting

Hearing aids repairs and baterries change

Our hearing aid repair specialists are trained to deal with specific hearing aid repair issues and have access to the parts and equipment needed to get your hearing aids working properly again. We partner with major hearing aid manufacturers for training and technical support for their specific hearing aid models, and they supply us with parts. Simple repairs can be done while you wait, but there are times when we need a little extra time, or we have to send them to the manufacturer for repair. In both cases, we do our best to get them back in your ears ASAP, and we will provide you with a loaner pair while you wait.

You should bring your hearing aids for repairs if you experience any of following problems:

  • Replacing batteries more frequently
  • See cracks, breaks, holes or other damage to your hearing aid
  • Whistling or unpleasant noises while wearing your hearing aids
